Jrnl - Manage your notes and journal entries from Command line

Tuesday, Dec 10, 2024

Making alias for @jrnl - j in my zsh. Earlier it was aliased to ‘jobs-l’. Basic Usage jrnl has two modes: composing and viewing. Basically, whenever you don’t supply any arguments that start with a dash or double-dash, you’re in composing mode, meaning you can write your entry on the command line or an editor of your choice.

We intentionally break a convention on command line arguments: all arguments starting with a single dash will filter your journal before viewing it, and can be combined arbitrarily. Arguments with a double dash will control how your journal is displayed or exported and are mutually exclusive (ie. you can only specify one way to display or export your journal at a time).
